Italian Pasta

Below is a step by step guide to Prepared this top notch dish

Ingredients

  • Protein: Lean ground beef is standard, often mixed with ground pork or pancetta for added richness.
  • Vegetable Base (Soffritto): A finely diced mixture of onion, carrot, and celery is the traditional base for the sauce.
  • Aromatics: Garlic is a common addition, as are olive oil and sometimes butter for sautéing.
  • Canned tomatoes (chopped, crushed, or passata) provide the main body of the sauce.
  • Wine (red or white) is often used to deglaze the pan and add acidity.
  • Seasoning & Herbs: Salt, black pepper, dried oregano, thyme, and bay leaves are typical. Worcestershire sauce is also sometimes added for a savoury kick.
  • Spaghetti pasta.
  • Parmesan cheese for grating over the top
  • Fresh basil or parsley for garnish.

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium heat. Add the finely diced onion, carrot, and celery (known as a soffritto base), and minced garlic. Sauté for about 5 to 10 minutes until the vegetables have softened and become fragrant but not browned.
  2. Brown the Meat: Increase the heat to medium-high. Add the ground beef (and pork, if using), breaking it apart with a wooden spoon as it cooks. Cook until the meat is fully browned and no pink remains. Drain any excess grease if necessary.
  3. Deglaze the Pan: Pour in the red or white wine (if using) and bring it to a simmer. Scrape any browned bits from the bottom of the pan and cook for a minute or two until the alcohol smell has cooked off
  4. Add Remaining Ingredients & Simmer: Stir in the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, beef stock, dried herbs (oregano, basil, thyme, bay leaves), Worcestershire sauce, salt, and pepper. Bring the mixture to a simmer, then reduce the heat to low, cover partially, and let it simmer gently. The longer it simmers, the richer the flavour; aim for at least 20-30 minutes, but some recipes call for 1-2 hours or more. Stir occasionally and add water or stock as needed if it becomes too thick.
  5. Adjust Seasoning: Taste the sauce and adjust the salt and pepper as needed after simmering.
  6. Cook the Pasta: While the sauce simmers, bring a large pot of salted water to a rolling boil. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente (firm to the bite)
  7. Combine and Serve: Drain the spaghetti. You can either combine the spaghetti with the sauce in the pot or serve the pasta in a bowl with a generous ladle of Bolognese sauce on top.
  8. Garnish: Sprinkle generously with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and a garnish of fresh basil leaves, then serve hot.
